# Customer Retention & Revenue Leakage Analysis

## Overview

This end-to-end analytics project examines how customer retention impacts long-term revenue sustainability in an e-commerce business. While total revenue appears strong, deeper analysis reveals that the majority of revenue is generated by one-time customers, exposing significant revenue leakage caused by poor repeat purchase behavior.

Using Python, SQL, and Power BI, the project transforms raw transactional data into actionable business insights through customer segmentation, cohort retention analysis, revenue trend monitoring, and leakage diagnostics.

---

## Business Problem

High revenue can create the illusion of a healthy business. However, if customers do not return after their first purchase, the company becomes heavily dependent on constant customer acquisition.

This project answers:

- Is revenue sustainable?
- How many customers return after their first purchase?
- How much revenue depends on one-time buyers?
- Where is customer lifetime value being lost?

---

## Key Findings

- Total Revenue exceeded **15.8M**
- Total Customers exceeded **96K**
- Repeat Purchase Rate was only **~3%**
- Over **94% of revenue** came from one-time customers
- Customer retention dropped sharply after the first purchase across nearly all cohorts
- Revenue growth was strong, but highly dependent on new customer acquisition

## Business Impact

This analysis demonstrates that strong top-line revenue does not always indicate business health. Poor retention can silently erode customer lifetime value and force unsustainable acquisition spending.

The project provides a framework for identifying retention gaps and prioritizing strategies such as:

- Loyalty programs
- Personalized re-engagement campaigns
- Lifecycle email flows
- Customer experience improvements
- Retention-focused marketing optimization
